Get Out West

Proposed interactive experience that drives outdoor adventures.

challenge

Our client approached us to drive engagement for their new program that seeks to motivate families to get outdoors, explore and create adventures.

solution

Our research showed that the barrier to families engaging the outdoors included where to go, concerns with cost, what activities were available and how difficult were these activities. In response we developed an integrated map, gamification and travel journal system that helps to answer each of these concerns.

the result

Our proposed system was met with great excitement within the committee and throughout the entire organization. We are currently awaiting decisions on next steps and a possible start date.

my role

For this proposal I acted as UX architect and technologist to determine the best solutions and the most appropriate technologies for implementation.

brainstorm

Starting from our initial ideas of solution components, we brainstorm requirements for each part of the system, validating it against the overall objectives. One of our main areas of focus was balancing the user needs of the children with the concerns of their parents.

wireframes

Working with pen and paper allows rapid development of screen wireframes for the mapping system, the mobile gamification system and the adventure journal photo album.

adventure map

Allows families to easily locate outdoor activities near and far. They can easily sort activities by type, difficulty, cost and other factors.

activity journal

This live journal automatically combines the family's on-site photography with aggregated content about the location and wild-life. This journal also tracks the children's activity badge achievements and progress.
